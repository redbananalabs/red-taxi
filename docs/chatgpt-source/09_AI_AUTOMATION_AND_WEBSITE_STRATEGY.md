# AI Automation and Website Strategy

## Strategic fit
This platform is stronger when paired with white-label taxi websites and SEO landing pages that generate direct bookings.

## Website -> dispatch pipeline
1. User lands on taxi route or area page.
2. User requests quote or books.
3. Booking data posts into dispatch platform.
4. Operator confirms or assisted automation handles initial flow.
5. Booking enters dispatch queue.

## AI opportunities
### Near-term / realistic
- AI-assisted booking summarisation from call notes
- AI quote drafting for unusual jobs
- operator copilots for customer support
- anomaly detection on failed assignments
- natural language reporting queries

### Mid-term
- AI voice phone booking intake
- route-based pricing recommendations
- demand forecasting
- dispatch recommendation explanations

### Longer-term
- autonomous booking triage
- AI-powered peak scheduling suggestions
- proactive partner coverage recommendation

## Product requirement
AI should be layered in as optional capabilities, not hard dependencies for v1.
This keeps launch simpler and commercially safer.

## Why this matters commercially
Combining:
- lead generation websites,
- booking conversion,
- dispatch fulfilment,
- reporting,
creates a stronger SaaS than dispatch alone.
