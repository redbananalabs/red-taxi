# Packaging and Pricing Model

## Why packaging matters
This SaaS will be used by very different operators.

Example:
A small rural operator may have about 20 drivers on/off and around 100 bookings daily.
A larger firm may have many more drivers, operator users, API integrations, and partner relationships.

So pricing should not be based on one simplistic metric only.

## Recommended entitlement dimensions
- active drivers,
- monthly bookings,
- operator/admin seats,
- number of vehicles,
- partner network features,
- API access,
- advanced analytics,
- AI automation features.

## Suggested commercial structure
### Starter
For smaller owner-managed fleets.
- limited active drivers
- limited monthly bookings
- core booking + dispatch
- basic reporting

### Growth
For established local operators.
- higher driver cap
- higher booking volume
- pricing rules
- notifications
- driver app
- basic integrations

### Professional
For multi-operator or regional businesses.
- much higher limits
- partner-company job sharing
- advanced reporting
- account workflows
- priority support

### Enterprise
For larger networks or bespoke needs.
- custom limits
- custom onboarding
- dedicated support
- advanced integrations
- special commercial terms

## Optional add-ons
- extra booking volume packs
- extra operator seats
- branded booking widgets
- AI phone answering
- advanced analytics
- partner settlement automation

## Internal product requirement
The domain and entitlement model should support:
- plan,
- meter/usage counters,
- soft/hard limits,
- feature flags,
- upgrade prompts.
